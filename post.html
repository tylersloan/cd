Prevent costly repairs with these 20 boat storage tips

Jonathan McWriter

Bad things can happen to an RV when it's not stored properly. After you've scrimped and saved, have purchased a motorhome or camper, and have finally begun your dream of RVing, the last thing you want is to have your RV spoiled by damage that could have been prevented. And RV deterioration is expensive! Up to $5000 in repair costs can result from mold, pests, and system damage. Poor storage practices introduce risks you just don't want to take.

Preventing costly damage during the off season is worth the time you'll need to spend doing it, and simply involves taking several steps to prepare and protect your RV. This article shares twenty RV storage tips that, if followed, should save you serious cash.

1. Clean and Dry the RV Exterior

First, wash and wax the exterior of your RV. A thorough washing (including cleaning the undercarriage) will take off any abrasive grime that could damage the paint and metal, and waxing will protect the finish. Follow the wash and wax by drying all remaining water off, to keep moisture issues from developing.

2. Use a RV Cover

Roofs are particularly vulnerable to weather hazards like sun, rain, and snow. A breathable RV cover will prevent water leaks and costly roof repairs. Covering will also preserve the overall condition of the RV and will dramatically reduce the dreaded black streaks on the exterior that tend to appear during storage. You’ll get the best results from your RV cover if you make sure to apply it so it’s completely sheltering all parts of the RV.

20. Have Someone Check On Your RV Regularly

Even if your RV is locked up, you'll feel better if you have a friend or family member checking on it occasionally. Whoever is looking after your RV can also keep an eye on any issues that may crop up, such as pests or mildew, and get them addressed before they cause real damage.

When the storage season is over and you're ready to get back on the road, you'll be ready to roll if you've followed these preventative steps. And the money you would have spent on repairs can be spent on anything from new RV accessories to a National Park Pass!
